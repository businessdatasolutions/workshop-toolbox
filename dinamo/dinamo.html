Theoretical Underpinnings of the DINAMO Framework

The DINAMO framework is a validated instrument designed to assess an individual’s willingness to change – a key factor in successful organizational change. It is rooted in Ajzen’s Theory of Planned Behavior, which proposes that behavior is driven by behavioral intentions that are, in turn, influenced by:

  • Attitudes: How one cognitively and affectively evaluates the change.
  • Subjective Norms: Perceived social pressures regarding the change.
  • Perceived Behavioral Control: One’s confidence in the ability to implement the change.

The DINAMO inventory operationalizes these components using validated questionnaire items (see Metselaar, 1997 and subsequent studies). By measuring each element, the tool provides a comprehensive picture of one’s overall willingness to invest time and effort in supporting change.

DINAMO Questionnaire

Please indicate your level of agreement with each statement (1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly Agree):

1. I believe that the proposed change will improve my work performance.

2. My colleagues support the change initiative.

3. I feel confident in my ability to implement the necessary changes.

4. I am willing to invest the necessary time and effort to support the change.
